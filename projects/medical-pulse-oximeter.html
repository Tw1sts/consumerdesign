MediPulse Oximeter

A complete redesign of a professional-grade pulse oximeter for improved usability in high-stress medical environments. This project focused on creating a device that could be operated correctly with minimal attention, even in emergency situations.

Client: MediTech Instruments

Year: 2024

Role: Lead Product Designer

Deliverables: Research Report, Design Specifications, Production Documentation, Usability Testing

The Challenge

MediTech's existing pulse oximeter was technically accurate but had significant usability issues that were causing problems in clinical settings:

  • Complex menu system required too much attention
  • Small display was difficult to read in low-light conditions
  • Physical design was uncomfortable during extended use
  • Battery replacement was difficult with gloved hands
  • Alarms were frequently silenced due to confusing controls

Research Approach

We conducted a comprehensive research program to understand the contexts and needs of medical professionals:

  • 24 hours of observational shadowing in ER, ICU, and general ward settings
  • Interviews with 18 nurses, doctors, and respiratory therapists
  • Contextual inquiry sessions with both experienced and new users
  • Competitive analysis of 8 similar devices
  • Error analysis from incident reports

Key Insights

Our research revealed several critical insights that guided the redesign:

  1. Users rarely read device screens directly – they listen for alarms and glance at numbers from a distance
  2. In emergency situations, fine motor control decreases significantly
  3. Devices are often operated one-handed while the user is performing other tasks
  4. High-contrast displays are essential in varied lighting conditions
  5. The most common tasks should be accessible without menu navigation

Design Solution

We redesigned the MediPulse from the ground up with these key features:

  • Simplified Interface: Reduced the UI to show only essential information with large, high-contrast numbers
  • Direct Controls: Dedicated physical buttons for the most common tasks, eliminating menu navigation
  • Ergonomic Form: Asymmetric design with a textured grip that works with medical gloves
  • Visual + Tactile Feedback: Color-coded alarms with distinctive vibration patterns for different alerts
  • Quick-Change Battery: Redesigned battery compartment that can be opened with one hand while wearing gloves
  • Cleanability: Sealed seams and antimicrobial coating for easy sterilization

Testing & Iteration

We tested the design through multiple iterations:

  1. Paper prototypes to validate the core interface concept
  2. 3D-printed form models to refine the ergonomics
  3. Interactive prototypes tested in simulated emergency scenarios
  4. Final validation with 30 medical professionals across three hospitals

Each round of testing led to refinements, with particular attention to ensuring the device could be operated correctly under stress and with minimal visual attention.

Results

The redesigned MediPulse has been enthusiastically received by medical professionals and has delivered significant improvements:

  • 99.8% task completion rate in user testing, up from 88% with the previous design
  • Training time reduced by 68%
  • Critical error rate decreased by 91% in simulated emergency scenarios
  • Product Net Promoter Score increased from 22 to 76
  • Won a Gold Medical Design Excellence Award in 2024

Lessons Learned

This project reinforced several important principles for medical device design:

  • Designing for the extremes of use is critical – what works in calm conditions may fail under stress
  • Direct, unambiguous feedback is essential for critical devices
  • Physical form and digital interface must be considered as an integrated system
  • Testing in context reveals issues that controlled usability tests might miss
